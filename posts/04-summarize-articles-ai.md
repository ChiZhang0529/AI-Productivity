# How to Summarize Long Articles with AI for Free

You have 47 bookmarks. Maybe 147. Most of them are articles you meant to read "when you have time"—which, let's be honest, means never. AI can help you extract the key points from those long reads without actually reading every word.

## Why Summarize Instead of Reading

Not everything deserves deep attention. Industry news, competitor analysis, research roundups—these often contain one or two useful ideas buried in thousands of words. Summarization lets you decide if the full article is worth your time.

The key: use summaries to triage, not replace reading. If a summary shows genuine depth or relevance, read the original.

## Free Tools That Actually Work

### ChatGPT (Free Tier)

The free version of ChatGPT handles article summarization well, especially for text-heavy content.

**How to use it:**

1. Copy the article text
2. Paste it into ChatGPT
3. Ask: "Summarize this article in 300 words, focusing on the main argument and key takeaways"

Works best with articles under 3,000 words. Longer pieces may get truncated.

### Gemini (Google's AI)

Gemini handles longer content more reliably and can access URLs directly if you share the link.

**Prompt example:**

> "Read this article and give me: 1) A 200-word summary, 2) Three actionable insights, 3) One point I should research further"

### Claude (Free Tier Available)

Claude tends to preserve more nuance in summaries and works well for technical or academic content.

## Step-by-Step: Summarizing an Article

### Step 1: Find the Core Message

Before summarizing, identify what you actually want from this article. Are you looking for:

- The main argument?
- Practical takeaways?
- Data and statistics?
- Counterarguments?

Your goal changes what the summary should emphasize.

### Step 2: Paste and Prompt

Don't just paste and say "summarize." Give specific instructions:

> "I'm a [your background]. Summarize this article focusing on [your interest area]. Include any data points or examples that support the main argument."

### Step 3: Extract Actionable Points

Most summaries stop at "here's what it said." Better prompt:

> "Based on this article, what are three specific things I could do differently?"

### Step 4: Save With Context

When you save the summary, add a note: "Why I saved this" and "What I'll do with it." Otherwise, you end up with a graveyard of summaries you won't remember.

## A Real Example

A marketing manager I know needed to stay current on content marketing trends but spent her commute time scrolling social media instead of reading. She started using AI summaries.

Her process: Sunday evenings, she picks 5 bookmarked articles, pastes each into ChatGPT, and asks for a 150-word summary plus "one thing I could try this week." She keeps a running document called "Weekly Insights."

After two months, she had 40+ actionable ideas and actually remembered them because she wrote down what she'd do with each one. "I went from fake-reading to actually learning," she said.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake:** Relying entirely on auto-summarizers (tools that summarize webpages automatically). These often miss context and produce generic output.

**Better approach:** Copy-paste the text yourself and write your own prompt. Takes 2 extra minutes, produces 10x better results.

**Mistake:** Summarizing without intention. "Summarize this" gives you someone else's idea of what's important. "Summarize this for someone who needs quick wins in project management" gives you something actually useful.

## When NOT to Summarize

Some content needs full attention:

- Legal documents or contracts
- Technical tutorials you need to follow step-by-step
- Creative writing or long-form journalism you're reading for pleasure
- Anything where understanding the author's reasoning matters

Summarize news and informational content. Read deeply when nuance and reasoning matter.

## Quick Template

```
Article: [Title/URL]
Why I want to read it: [Your goal]
Main point: [AI's answer]
Key takeaways:
1.
2.
3.
One thing I'll try/do differently:
```

Save this template somewhere. Use it consistently. Your future self will thank you.
