# Free AI Video Script Generator Tips

You need to make a video. You've got the idea, maybe even the footage. But the script? You're staring at a blank document for 30 minutes before giving up and hoping the camera will figure it out out. It won't. AI can help you write video scripts faster—if you know how to use it.

## Why Video Scripts Matter

Skipping the script feels efficient. You're wrong. Without a script:

- Videos ramble and lose viewers
- Key points get missed
- You waste time doing multiple takes
- Editing takes three times longer

A 60-second video with a script takes 30 minutes to film. The same video without a script? Two hours, minimum.

## The Three Types of Video Scripts

Before prompting, know what you need:

1. **Short-form (under 60 seconds):** Hook → Point → Call to action
2. **Medium-form (1-5 minutes):** Hook → Context → Main points (3 max) → Takeaway
3. **Long-form (5+ minutes):** Full structure with sections, transitions, and signposting

AI helps with all three, but requires different prompts for each.

## Step-by-Step: Writing a Script with AI

### Step 1: Define the Video's Purpose

Ask yourself:

- What should viewers understand or do after watching?
- What's the single most important message?
- What's the call to action?

Write this down. Share it with AI.

### Step 2: Choose Your Structure

For a 60-second video about productivity:

> "Write a script for a 60-second video about [your topic]. Structure: Hook (first 5 seconds that grabs attention), Main point (what you want them to remember), Call to action (what you want them to do). Target audience: [description]. Tone: [conversational/energetic/professional]."

### Step 3: Get the First Draft

Give AI detailed input:

- Your main talking points
- Any statistics or facts you want to include
- Examples you want to mention
- Your preferred tone
- Video length

### Step 4: Make It Speakable

AI often writes sentences that read well but don't flow when spoken. After getting your draft:

> "This script is good but some lines feel awkward when spoken. Make it conversational—short sentences, natural word order, like I'm talking to a friend."

### Step 5: Add Timing Markers

> "Add timing markers to each section. Total runtime should be approximately [X] seconds."

## A Real Example

A small business owner was making Instagram Reels about her bakery. She had great footage of bread baking, cakes decorating, satisfied customers. But her videos felt awkward—long pauses, rambling explanations, awkward transitions.

We worked on her script approach:

**Before (her method):** Wing it, hope for the best, delete the worst takes

**After (scripted approach):**
- Hook: "The secret to perfect sourdough isn't in the recipe."
- Point: Explain the one technique that changed her bread
- CTA: "Try it this weekend and tell me how it goes"

First scripted video: 3 takes to film, 15 minutes total. Her comments exploded compared to her winging-it videos.

"The script isn't making me robotic," she said. "It's giving me confidence because I know exactly what to say."

## Useful Video Script Prompts

### For a quick explainer:
> "Write a 90-second video script that explains [concept] to [audience]. Start with a relatable problem, then reveal the solution. End with [specific CTA]."

### For a product demo:
> "Write a 2-minute product demo script for [product]. Include: what it does, how it solves the problem, one key feature, and price/where to buy."

### For educational content:
> "Write a 3-minute educational script about [topic]. Use an example [relevant to audience], explain why it matters, and give actionable takeaway."

### For a vlog-style intro:
> "Write a 30-second vlog intro in a [energetic/chill/excited] tone. Cover: what we'll do today, why you're watching, and a hook for the main content."

## Common Video Script Mistakes (And How AI Helps Fix Them)

### Mistake: Too much information
AI fix: "Shorten this to fit a 60-second read-aloud. Cut anything non-essential."

### Mistake: Too formal
AI fix: "Make this conversational. Pretend you're explaining this to a friend over coffee."

### Mistake: No clear CTA
AI fix: "This script doesn't have a call to action. Add one at the end that tells viewers exactly what to do next."

### Mistake: Opening doesn't grab attention
AI fix: "The current opening is generic. Write 5 alternative hooks—each different, each attention-grabbing in a different way."

## Adding Your Personality

AI writes competent scripts. You make them yours. After AI drafts:

1. Replace one statistic with a personal story
2. Add a phrase you'd actually say
3. Adjust the tone to match your voice
4. Insert a joke or aside if that's your style

The script is a foundation. Your personality makes it real.

## Quick Template for Any Video

```
HOOK (0-10 seconds):
[Attention-grabbing opener]

CONTEXT (10-20 seconds):
[Brief setup—why this matters now]

MAIN CONTENT (20-50 seconds):
[Point 1]
[Point 2 - if needed]
[Point 3 - if needed]

TAKEOUT (50-55 seconds):
[One thing you want them to remember]

CALL TO ACTION (55-60 seconds):
[What you want them to do next]
```

Fill in each section with your content, use AI to draft each section, then stitch together.

## Final Tips

1. Write for ears, not eyes—read everything aloud before filming
2. Shorter is better—70% of viewers leave in the first 10 seconds
3. First 3 seconds are everything—your hook determines whether they stay
4. One message per video—don't try to cover everything

AI gets you to a usable first draft. You bring the personality that keeps people watching.
