# How to Avoid AI Writing Sounding Robotic

You've seen it. That slightly off quality—too formal, too perfect, too many buzzwords. AI-generated text has a recognizable fingerprint, and readers can usually sense it even when they can't explain why. Here's how to fix that.

## Why AI Writing Sounds Artificial

AI optimizes for "correct." It produces grammatically perfect, logically structured text with no personality quirks, no sentence fragments, no casual asides. But human writing isn't like that. Human writing has rhythm, preference, contradiction, and voice.

The fix isn't complicated, but it requires effort: you need to add friction, imperfection, and personality back in.

## The Five Biggest Giveaways (And Fixes)

### 1. Generic Transitions

AI loves: "Furthermore," "Additionally," "In conclusion," "It is important to note that."

Humans say: "But here's the thing," "Which brings us to," "So basically," "Look, the real issue is..."

**Fix:** After writing, search for these filler phrases. Replace with conversational alternatives.

### 2. Hedging Everything

AI never says "This is definitely wrong" or "You should always do X." It hedges: "Generally speaking," "In most cases," "It may be beneficial to consider..."

**Fix:** Take a stance. Replace "It could be helpful" with "Do this." Replace "Some experts suggest" with "Here's what works."

### 3. Lists When Narrative Would Work

AI loves bullet points because they're structured. But lists feel corporate, not human.

**Fix:** Convert some bullets into sentences, anecdotes, or questions. "Common mistakes include: A, B, C" becomes "Most people run into problems with A first. Then comes B. And C sneaks up when you least expect it."

### 4. Over-Explaining

AI assumes the reader knows nothing and explains everything. "Coffee is a popular beverage made from roasted beans."

**Fix:** Trust your reader. If you're explaining something obvious to your audience, cut it.

### 5. Perfect Paragraph Length

AI produces paragraphs of nearly identical length. Humans vary rhythm constantly.

**Fix:** Mix it up. Some paragraphs one sentence. Some paragraphs five sentences. Let breathing room vary.

## Step-by-Step: Humanizing Your AI Draft

### Step 1: Read It Out Loud

This is the single most effective technique. If you stumble reading it, it sounds robotic. If you can't imagine saying it to a friend, it sounds robotic.

### Step 2: Add One Personal Touch

Add a quick aside, a real example, or a sentence that shows personality:

> Original: "Regular exercise improves mental health."
> Humanized: "Regular exercise improves mental health—I started running three mornings a week last year, and honestly? My therapist noticed before I did."

### Step 3: Delete the First Paragraph

AI often writes generic introductions. The real content starts at paragraph two. Try deleting the intro entirely and starting with the second or third paragraph.

### Step 4: Vary Sentence Starts

Read your first sentence of each paragraph. If they all start with "It," "This," "AI," or "One," vary them.

## A Real Example

A blogger I follow used AI to draft posts for months. Her engagement tanked. Comments dropped. She couldn't figure out why—her posts were well-structured, informative, grammatically correct.

She asked readers what changed. One replied: "It feels like reading a Wikipedia article. Informative but... nothing sticks. Your old posts felt like you were talking to me."

She started running drafts through a simple checklist:

1. Read aloud—where did I stumble?
2. Circle every buzzword and vague claim
3. Add one sentence with a real experience
4. Delete the intro if it didn't pull me in

Her engagement recovered within six weeks. Same information, same structure, but with her voice restored.

## Quick Before/After Examples

| AI-sounding | Human-sounding |
|------------|----------------|
| "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, it is imperative that businesses adapt their strategies accordingly." | "Businesses that ignore digital are falling behind. Fast." |
| "Furthermore, regular exercise has been shown to improve mental well-being." | "Exercise helps your brain. That's not opinion—that's research." |
| "It is worth noting that this approach may not be suitable for all individuals." | "This won't work for everyone. But for most people? Worth trying." |
| "In conclusion, there are several key takeaways to consider." | "Here's what matters:" |

## The Real Secret

There's no button that makes text human. The process is:

1. Use AI for structure and first drafts
2. Inject your perspective, voice, and experience
3. Read it like you're talking to a friend
4. Edit until it sounds like you

AI writes like a very competent, very boring consultant. Your job is to make it sound like you.
