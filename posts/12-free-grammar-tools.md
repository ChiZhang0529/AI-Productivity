# Free AI Grammar Check Tools for Bloggers

Every blogger makes typos. The difference between a polished post and an embarrassing one often comes down to proofreading—but most bloggers are too close to their own work to catch mistakes. AI grammar tools solve this by seeing your text with fresh eyes.

## Why Bloggers Need Dedicated Grammar Tools

Your word processor's spell-check catches obvious errors. But grammar tools catch the subtle issues that make writing feel amateur:

- Sentences that run on too long
- Passive voice overuse
- Inconsistent tense
- Awkward phrasing that technically isn't wrong but feels off
- Readability issues for your target audience

## Top Free Options

### 1. Grammarly Free

**Best for:** General bloggers who want comprehensive checking

Grammarly catches the most error types and offers the clearest explanations. The browser extension works in Gmail, Google Docs, WordPress editor, and most text fields online.

**What it catches well:**

- Subject-verb agreement
- Article usage (a/an/the confusion)
- Punctuation inside quotes
- Plagiarism (handy for avoiding accidental duplication)

**Limitation:** Free version doesn't catch style issues, only errors.

### 2. Hemingway App

**Best for:** Making your writing bold and clear

Hemingway highlights complex sentences, adverbs, and passive voice. It's less about "correct" and more about "readable."

**What it does well:**

- Highlights sentences above grade 10 reading level
- Shows exactly where readability drops
- Forces you to simplify

**Limitation:** Doesn't catch all grammar errors, only style issues.

### 3. LanguageTool

**Best for:** Multilingual bloggers or those wanting open-source options

LanguageTool catches errors across English, German, French, Spanish, and dozens of other languages. Good if your audience is international.

**What it does well:**

- Multiple language support
- Chrome and Firefox extensions
- API available for developers

**Limitation:** Interface is less polished than Grammarly.

### 4. Wordtune

**Best for:** Improving sentence-level writing

Wordtune doesn't just catch errors—it suggests rewrites. You can ask: "Make this more formal" or "Shorten this" and it provides alternatives.

**What it does well:**

- Rewrite suggestions
- Tone adjustment
- Spicing up boring sentences

**Limitation:** Less emphasis on catching errors; more on improving style.

## A Practical Workflow

### Before You Publish (Minimum)

1. Write your draft
2. Run it through Grammarly (free browser extension)
3. Fix highlighted errors
4. Run it through Hemingway
5. Simplify highlighted passages
6. Read aloud—one more time
7. Publish

Total extra time: 5-10 minutes. Value: immeasurable.

### Advanced (If You Have Time)

Add Wordtune into step 5: after Hemingway simplifies, ask Wordtune to "spice up" key sentences you don't want to lose punch from.

## A Real Example

A food blogger I follow had excellent recipes but consistently got comments about "sloppy writing." Recipes were great; intros and instructions felt rushed and error-prone.

She installed Grammarly and committed to running every post through before publishing. First week, it caught 47 errors across 5 posts. Most were minor—missing commas, occasional "your/you're" confusion—but some were significant clarity issues.

Six months later, her comment section shifted from "great recipe, but..." to genuine compliments about the writing. She's still a home cook, not a professional writer—but her posts now sound like she could be.

"I just needed someone to notice the typos I couldn't see," she said. "Grammarly doesn't write better, but it points out what needs fixing."

## Common Blogging Errors These Tools Catch

| Error Type | Example | Caught By |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| "Your/You're" confusion | "Your the best" | Grammarly |
| Missing commas | "However I disagree" | Grammarly |
| Long sentences | "We then discussed which then led to..." | Hemingway |
| Passive voice | "The cake was eaten by us" | Hemingway, Grammarly |
| Repeated words | "The the problem" | Grammarly |

## Integration Options

**Browser extensions:** Work everywhere you write online
**Desktop apps:** Better for long-form content
**Google Docs add-ons:** If you draft in Docs
**WordPress plugins:** Direct integration with your CMS

Install at least one. The goal is to catch errors before readers do.

## Quick Tip: Read It Backward

After running your post through tools, try reading it backward—last sentence first, then second-to-last, etc. This forces your brain to focus on words, not meaning, and you'll catch typos your mind skipped over.

Grammar tools catch most errors. This trick catches the rest.
