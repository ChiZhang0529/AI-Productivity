AI Tools to Speed Up Email Writing

AI Productivity Editorial

The average office worker spends 28% of their workday on email. That's nearly three hours every day typing, editing, and re-reading messages. AI can't eliminate email, but it can significantly cut the time you spend on each one.

The Two Types of Email Problems

Before choosing a tool, identify your problem:

Type 1: Blank page paralysis — You know what you want to say but can't get started.

Type 2: Slow drafting — You can write emails fine, but they take too long to get right.

Different problems need different solutions.

Free Tools That Work

ChatGPT for Blank Page Problems

When you're stuck staring at a blank screen:

  1. Write 2-3 bullet points of what you need to communicate
  2. Paste them into ChatGPT
  3. Ask: "Write a professional email based on these points: [your bullets]"

This is most effective for emails where you know the substance but struggle with phrasing—client updates, project status reports, meeting requests.

Example prompt:

"Write a professional email to my team explaining that we're pushing the launch date by two weeks. Keep it positive, acknowledge their hard work, and give the new timeline clearly."

The AI gives you a starting point. You adjust tone, add specifics, and send.

Grammarly for Slow Drafting

Grammarly's free version now includes tone suggestions and rewrite options. Write your email normally, then use "Coaching" suggestions to tighten clarity.

The key feature: it shows you how your email might be perceived—"This sounds more urgent than intended" or "This could come across as passive-aggressive."

Gemini in Gmail

If you're on Google Workspace, Gemini can draft responses directly in Gmail. Open an email, click "Help me write," and it generates a response based on context.

This works best for routine replies: meeting confirmations, acknowledgment messages, follow-up questions.

Step-by-Step: Email Workflow with AI

Step 1: Quick Sort

Before writing anything, categorize your email:

  • Needs full response (3+ minutes to write properly)
  • Can use template (30-60 seconds)
  • AI can help draft (1-2 minutes with AI assistance)

Only use AI for emails that justify the effort. Don't AI-draft a one-line reply.

Step 2: Draft with AI or Without?

For routine emails with clear substance, use AI:

"Draft an email to [client] explaining that their order ships tomorrow and providing the tracking number once available."

For sensitive emails (rejection letters, difficult conversations, anything emotionally charged): write yourself, then use AI only for grammar checking. AI can flatten emotional nuance in ways that feel cold.

Step 3: Personalize the Draft

AI drafts are starting points. Add:

  • Specific names or references ("As we discussed in Tuesday's call...")
  • Your personality ("I'm excited about this too, which is why...")
  • Anything the AI couldn't know ("Great question—here's why we made that choice...")

The more you personalize, the less "AI-sounding" your email becomes.

A Real Example

A sales rep I work with used to spend 90 minutes each morning responding to overnight emails. He identified his bottleneck: routine client updates and follow-up messages.

He now uses a simple system: for each email, he writes bullet points in his notes app, then pastes them into ChatGPT with a template:

"Write a [friendly/professional/enthusiastic] email with these points: [bullets]. Keep it under 150 words."

He reviews the output, adjusts 1-2 sentences, and sends. His morning email time dropped to 40 minutes, and his response quality actually improved because he was no longer rushing through tired eyes.

Quick Templates You Can Use

Status update:

"Write a brief email updating [name] on [project]. Key points: [what happened], [what's next], [any blockers]. Keep it scannable with bullets."

Meeting request:

"Write a professional email requesting a 30-minute call with [name] to discuss [topic]. Suggest two specific times and mention why this is time-sensitive."

Follow-up:

"Write a friendly follow-up email to [name] about [topic we discussed]. I haven't heard back in [timeframe]. Keep it brief and not pushy."

What Not to Automate

Never use AI for:

  • Confidential information or anything requiring legal review
  • Emails when you're upset (AI can't calibrate emotional tone properly)
  • Replies that need your specific knowledge or judgment

The goal is to handle routine emails faster so you have more time for the ones that actually need your attention.
