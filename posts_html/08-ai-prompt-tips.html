Best AI Prompt Tips for Better Results

The difference between mediocre AI outputs and excellent ones usually comes down to how you ask. Prompt engineering isn't about tricking the AI—it's about communicating clearly. Here's what actually works.

The Foundation: specificity beats cleverness

"Write something" gets you generic output. "Write a 150-word LinkedIn post announcing our team's Q3 product launch, targeting non-technical stakeholders, with a tone that's confident but not arrogant" gets you something usable.

The more context you provide, the less the AI has to guess. And when AI guesses, it usually goes safe and generic.

Tip 1: Define the Output Format

Don't just say "summarize this." Say "summarize this in three bullet points, each starting with a verb."

Don't just say "write a blog post." Say "write a 600-word blog post with an H2 headline, three H3 subheadings, and a call-to-action at the end."

Specifying format gives you structured results you can actually use.

Tip 2: Give Examples

One of the most powerful prompting techniques: show, don't just tell.

"Write social media captions in this style: [Example 1], [Example 2]. Now write a caption about [your topic]."

The AI learns your preferred tone and style from examples better than from description alone.

Tip 3: Define Your Audience

"Who is this for?" changes everything.

"Explain photosynthesis to a 10-year-old" gets you something completely different than "Explain photosynthesis to a college biology student."

The audience shapes vocabulary, depth, and framing.

Tip 4: Set Constraints

Boundaries produce creativity:

  • "Write under 200 words"
  • "Use only simple vocabulary"
  • "Don't mention price"
  • "Include three specific examples"

Constraints force the AI to make choices rather than defaulting to generic comprehensive responses.

Tip 5: Chain Your Requests

Don't try to get perfect output in one prompt. Build iteratively:

  1. Ask for ideas (without judgment)
  2. Pick the best direction
  3. Ask for a first draft
  4. Ask for specific improvements
  5. Refine until satisfied

This "conversation" approach gives you much better results than expecting perfection from a single prompt.

A Real Example

A content writer I know was struggling with product descriptions. Her prompts were too vague: "Write a product description for our water bottle."

We changed her approach. Instead of one generic prompt, she started chaining:

First prompt: "Generate three angles for a water bottle product description: one focused on athletes, one on eco-conscious consumers, one on commuters."

Second prompt: "Write a 100-word product description for the commuter angle. Highlight: fits in cup holders, keeps drinks cold 24 hours, leak-proof lid."

Third prompt: "Make the tone more conversational and add a sentence about how it fits into busy lifestyles."

The final description was 10x better than anything she'd get from a single generic prompt. And she got three different versions to choose from.

Common Prompt Mistakes

Mistake: Being too vague

"Write about marketing" → "Write about how small businesses can use social media to build customer loyalty on a budget of under $100/month"

Mistake: Contradicting yourself

"Write something creative but also make it professional and straightforward." Be clear about priorities.

Mistake: Forgetting to review

AI outputs are drafts. Always read and adjust before using them, especially for professional contexts.

Advanced Techniques

Role Prompting

"You are an experienced copywriter with 20 years in tech marketing. Write..."

Setting a role gives AI a perspective and expertise level to work from.

Negative Prompting

"Write this description but don't use the words 'innovative,' 'cutting-edge,' or 'revolutionary.'"

Telling AI what to avoid is often more effective than telling it what to include.

Multi-Part Prompts

Break complex requests into numbered steps:

"1. Analyze this data and identify the three biggest trends. 2. Explain why each trend matters to small business owners. 3. Suggest one action small businesses should take in response to each trend."

Quick Template for Complex Requests



Role: [Who should AI be acting as?]

Task: [What specifically do you need?]

Audience: [Who is this for?]

Tone/Style: [How should it sound?]

Format: [How should it be structured?]

Constraints: [Any limitations or requirements?]

Examples: [Optional - show preferred style]

The goal isn't to write elaborate prompts—it's to give AI enough context that it doesn't have to guess. Clarity in, quality out.
